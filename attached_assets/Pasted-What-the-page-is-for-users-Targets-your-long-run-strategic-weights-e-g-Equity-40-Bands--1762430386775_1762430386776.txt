What the page is (for users)

Targets = your long-run strategic weights (e.g., Equity 40%).

Bands = tolerance ranges around each target (e.g., 35–45%) to avoid over-trading.

Logic: if a bucket drifts outside its band, we propose trades to bring it back inside (by default to the mid-point), respecting wrappers, tax and costs.

Pasteable helper text (top of page, “ⓘ How it works”):

“This page tracks your strategic allocation. We watch drift vs. your target ranges and suggest low-friction actions (sell/buy/cash moves) that keep you in band while minimising tax and fees. Illiquid assets (e.g., property, private equity) are handled with gentle nudges, not trade orders.”

Data & calculations (simple, prototype-safe)

Drift = Current% – Target%.
Breach if Current% < BandMin OR > BandMax.
Trade amount to mid-point = (Mid% – Current%) × (Portfolio Value) restricted to eligible wrappers and eligible holdings.

Eligible holdings (v1)

Sell: liquid listed funds/ETFs/equities; inside wrappers first.

Buy: liquid listed funds/ETFs; cash source inside same wrapper if possible.

Property/PE: view-only nudges (“consider rebalancing after valuation”).

Acceptance criteria (so Replit knows when it’s done)

Header shows Overall drift, Rebalance rule, Review cadence.

Each bucket card displays Current/Target/Band, a drift chip, breakdown link, and Propose trades button.

View controls switch between Bucket / Wrapper / Account / With Liabilities (groups & subtotals change).

Rebalance Planner opens with a prefilled, editable table of suggested trades using the heuristics above and shows the post-trade percentages.

If any constituent asset has Valuation due, the bucket shows a pause state and the action button becomes Update valuation.

Saving a plan produces a plain-English summary (e.g., “Sell £7,200 of Global All-Cap in ISA; Buy £1,000 Cash”).

All copy uses UK English and matches your existing visual style.
