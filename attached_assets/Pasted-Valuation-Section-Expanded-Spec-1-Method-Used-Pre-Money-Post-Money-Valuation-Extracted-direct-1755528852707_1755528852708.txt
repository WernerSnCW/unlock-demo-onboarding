Valuation Section – Expanded Spec
1. Method Used

Pre-Money / Post-Money Valuation

Extracted directly from the deck (e.g. £20m pre-money).

Shows calculation with investment ask (e.g. £5m raise = £25m post-money).

Useful for quick dilution impact analysis.

Revenue Multiple (Comparable SaaS Benchmarking)

Applied to ARR or forecasted ARR.

E.g. £250k ARR × 10x = £2.5m fair value → compares against stated £20m.

Highlights overvaluation/undervaluation.

EBITDA Multiple (if later stage)

Applied if company projects positive EBITDA.

Shows whether valuation is in line with industry (e.g. 12–18x EBITDA for SaaS).

DCF (Discounted Cash Flow) (if data available)

Optional long-term value indicator.

2. Why This Method Is Used

Revenue Multiple: Most common for SaaS/tech startups at seed/series A. Focuses on recurring revenue potential.

EBITDA Multiple: Useful in later-stage businesses, reflects profitability and efficiency.

DCF: Captures long-term cashflow potential, but very assumption-heavy — used more for cross-checking.

ROI (Investor View): Quick way to see potential exit upside given ask + equity offered.

3. EBITDA View (Mock Example)

Forecasted EBITDA (Yr 3): £1.2m

Industry Average EBITDA Multiple: 12×

Implied Valuation: £14.4m

Deck Stated Valuation: £20m

Commentary: Implied deck valuation exceeds EBITDA-based benchmark, suggesting premium pricing.

4. ROI View (Mock Example)

Ask: £5m for 20% equity → £20m pre-money, £25m post-money.

Investor Equity Stake: 20% (assuming no dilution).

Projected Exit (Yr 5): £100m valuation.

Investor Return: £20m (20% of £100m).

ROI Multiple: 4×.

IRR (Internal Rate of Return): ~31% (over 5 years).

Commentary: Above VC target hurdle (~25–30%), but depends heavily on hitting ARR forecast.

6. Suggested Investor Questions

“Which valuation methodology did you use to justify £20m pre-money?”

“How do you reconcile the 8× difference between ARR multiple valuation (£2.5m) and your ask (£20m)?”

“What assumptions underpin your forecast EBITDA?”
