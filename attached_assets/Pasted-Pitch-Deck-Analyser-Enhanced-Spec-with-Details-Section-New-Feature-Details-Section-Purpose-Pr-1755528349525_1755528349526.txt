Pitch Deck Analyser – Enhanced Spec with Details Section
🔹 New Feature: Details Section
Purpose

Provide investors with a structured, section-by-section breakdown of the pitch deck. Each section shows:

Extracted content (text/numbers).

Commentary (strengths/weaknesses).

Suggested investor questions.

Benchmarks (where applicable).

🔹 Example Detailed Section Structure

1. Problem Statement

Extracted Content:
“SMEs lose £50bn annually due to inefficient invoice reconciliation.”

Strengths: Uses quantifiable pain point with credible source (PwC).

Weaknesses: No primary data or survey evidence provided.

Investor Questions:

“How did you validate the £50bn figure?”

“Have you spoken directly to SMEs to quantify demand?”

Benchmark: Problem slides typically score high if using third-party data + primary validation. Current deck = Medium strength.

2. Solution

Extracted Content:
“AI-powered reconciliation platform reduces processing time by 70%.”

Strengths: Clear and measurable benefit.

Weaknesses: No demo evidence, no IP/proprietary advantage shown.

Investor Questions:

“Is the AI model proprietary or built on third-party APIs?”

“What is the onboarding time for SMEs?”

Benchmark: Top solutions include live traction or demo screenshots. Deck = Needs evidence.

3. Market Size (TAM/SAM/SOM)

Extracted Content:
TAM: $50bn (PwC 2023)
SAM: $12bn (UK/EU mid-market)
SOM: $300m targetable in first 3 years.

Strengths: TAM + SAM included with source.

Weaknesses: SOM estimate unclear; no bottom-up methodology.

Investor Questions:

“How did you calculate your SOM — bottom-up or top-down?”

“What share of SOM is realistically achievable in 3 years?”

Benchmark: TAM always present, SOM often weak. Deck = Partial.

4. Business Model

Extracted Content:
SaaS subscription: £250/month SME, £1,200/month enterprise.

Strengths: Clear pricing tiers.

Weaknesses: No CAC or churn assumptions provided.

Investor Questions:

“What’s your projected CAC and payback period?”

“Do you anticipate different churn between SME vs enterprise?”

Benchmark: Investors expect CAC, LTV, churn. Deck = Light detail.

5. Traction

Extracted Content:
50 paying SMEs, £250k ARR, 15% MoM growth.

Strengths: Real revenue traction shown.

Weaknesses: Churn rate and pipeline missing.

Investor Questions:

“What’s your current churn rate?”

“Do you have signed LOIs with enterprises?”

Benchmark: Deck shows solid early traction. Deck = Above average.

6. Team

Extracted Content:
CEO: ex-Xero Head of Product
CTO: PhD in ML, ex-Google

Strengths: Experienced leadership.

Weaknesses: No commercial/finance hire mentioned.

Investor Questions:

“Who is responsible for fundraising and finance strategy?”

“Do you have advisors with SME industry expertise?”

Benchmark: Deck team is strong but incomplete.

7. Financials & Ask

Extracted Content:
Asking: £5m for 20% equity (pre-money £20m).
Forecast: £2m ARR in 18 months.

Strengths: Forecasted ARR shown.

Weaknesses: No bottom-up forecast detail; valuation high for stage.

Investor Questions:

“What assumptions underpin your £2m ARR forecast?”

“Why do you believe a £20m valuation is justified at seed stage?”

Benchmark: Valuation benchmark = £6–10m for UK SaaS seed. Deck = Overvalued.
