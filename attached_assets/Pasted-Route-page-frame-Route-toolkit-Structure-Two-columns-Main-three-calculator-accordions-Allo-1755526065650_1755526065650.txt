1) Allowance Calculator (EIS & SEIS) — Annual + carry-back
Inputs (grouped)

Scheme: EIS or SEIS (radio)

Is Knowledge-Intensive (KIC)? (EIS only) true | false

This tax year

investmentThisYear (£)

incomeTaxLiabilityThisYear (£) (cap relief to liability)

Previous tax year

investmentPrevYear (£ actually made last year)

unusedAllowancePrevYear (£, if known; otherwise tool derives from limits)

carryBackFromThisYear (£ user chooses how much of this year’s subscription to carry back)

incomeTaxLiabilityPrevYear (£) (cap carry-back relief)

Investor limit used in previous year? (checkbox) (if ticked, carry-back must be £0)

Limits to model

EIS: 30% relief, max £1m per tax year (£2m if KIC and at least £1m of total is in KICs). Carry-back allowed to the immediately preceding tax year, subject to that year’s limits and liability. 
GOV.UK
+1

SEIS: 50% relief, max £200k per tax year (from 6 Apr 2023). Carry-back allowed similarly, to the previous year within its limits. 
GOV.UK
+1

Calculations (deterministic)

Let:

rate = 0.30 for EIS; 0.50 for SEIS

limitThis = (scheme==EIS ? (isKIC? 2000000 : 1000000) : 200000)

limitPrev = same logic, but if shares issued in prev year use prev-year limit (we are modelling the investor limit; company rules out of scope)

Steps:

Validate carry-back cap
carryBackFromThisYear ≤ min(investmentThisYear, unusedAllowancePrevYear, limitPrev - investmentPrevYear)
If invalid, show inline error and cap automatically.

Relief base amounts

elegibleForPrev = min(carryBackFromThisYear, incomeTaxLiabilityPrevYear / rate) (cannot create a refund beyond tax due)

reliefPrev = elegibleForPrev × rate

remainingThisYearSubscription = investmentThisYear - carryBackFromThisYear

Apply this-year allowance

allowanceLeftThis = max(0, limitThis - remainingThisYearSubscription) (for info)

Cap relief to liability:
eligibleForThis = min(remainingThisYearSubscription, incomeTaxLiabilityThisYear / rate)
reliefThis = eligibleForThis × rate

Totals

totalRelief = reliefPrev + reliefThis

effectiveNetCost = (investmentThisYear) - (reliefThis) - (reliefPrev) (ignoring future disposal)

Validation & assumptions

Relief cannot exceed each year’s income tax liability (we do not model carry-forward of unused relief).

Carry-back is only to immediately preceding tax year.

We do not police company eligibility; this is an investor-limit calculator.

For EIS KIC, enforce: if limitThis == 2m, show helper text: “Anything above £1m must be in KICs.” 
GOV.UK

Outputs

Summary line: “Relief this year £X; carry-back relief £Y; total relief £Z.”

Breakdown panel (two columns): “This year” vs “Carried back to last year”

Subscription applied, Relief rate, Relief amount, Remaining allowance, Liability cap hit? (Yes/No)

Chips: EIS | SEIS, KIC (if applicable)

UX details

Show progress bars for allowance usage (this year / last year).

Inline cap badges if user hits a limit (allowance or liability).

Worked example (display under the card as “Example”)

EIS (non-KIC). Invest £120,000 this year; choose to carry back £20,000. Last year you used £950,000 of allowance and had £10,000 income tax due. This year you have £50,000 income tax due.

Last year remaining allowance = £50,000 → carry-back £20,000 ok. Relief last year = £20,000 × 30% = £6,000 (cap to liability if needed).

This year relief = (£120,000 − £20,000) × 30% = £30,000.

Total relief = £36,000. Remaining this-year allowance = £1,000,000 − £100,000 = £900,000.

2) Loss Relief Calculator — Downside modelling after upfront relief

Loss relief is available if the investment is disposed of at a loss. The loss is calculated on the effective cost after deducting the upfront income-tax relief, and can be set against income tax (marginal rate) or CGT, depending on the claim. 
Octopus Investments
Growth Capital Ventures
Wealth Club

Inputs

Scheme: EIS or SEIS (drives upfront rate: 30% vs 50%)

subscription (£)

saleProceeds (£) (0 if company fails)

Offset against: Income tax (choose marginal rate 20% / 40% / 45%) or CGT (10% / 20%)

(Optional) upfrontReliefActuallyClaimed (£) (default = scheme rate × subscription; editable for realism)

Calculations

Upfront relief
upfront = (upfrontReliefActuallyClaimed ?? subscription × rate) where rate = 0.30 (EIS) | 0.50 (SEIS)

Effective loss
effectiveLoss = max(0, subscription − upfront − saleProceeds)
(If proceeds > subscription − upfront, loss is £0.)

Loss relief

If offset against Income tax: lossRelief = effectiveLoss × marginalIncomeRate

If against CGT: lossRelief = effectiveLoss × chosenCGTRate

Net result
netLossAfterAllRelief = subscription − upfront − saleProceeds − lossRelief
Report also the % reduction vs gross loss:
grossLoss = subscription − saleProceeds
reductionPct = (1 − netLossAfterAllRelief / grossLoss) × 100

Validation & assumptions

We assume a qualifying loss and no disqualifying events.

We ignore disposal costs and time value of money.

If user changes upfrontReliefActuallyClaimed, clamp it to 0…(rate × subscription).

Show an info note: “Choosing CGT vs income offset depends on your circumstances; this tool does not optimise for you.”

Outputs

Headline: “Net loss after tax relief: £X (reduction of Y% vs gross loss)”

Breakdown table:

Subscription, Upfront relief, Proceeds, Effective loss, Chosen offset & rate, Loss relief value, Final net loss

Badge: EIS/SEIS, Offset: Income or Offset: CGT

Worked examples

EIS, 45% income taxpayer
Subscription £10,000; proceeds £0. Upfront 30% = £3,000.
Effective loss = £7,000. Loss relief = £7,000 × 45% = £3,150.
Net loss = £10,000 − £3,000 − £3,150 = £3,850 (61.5% reduction).

SEIS, 20% income taxpayer
Subscription £20,000; proceeds £5,000. Upfront 50% = £10,000.
Effective loss = £5,000 (20,000 − 10,000 − 5,000).
Loss relief = £5,000 × 20% = £1,000. Net loss = £4,000.

3) CGT Deferral Calculator — EIS reinvestment deferral + SEIS reinvestment (50% exemption)
What we model

EIS Deferral Relief: You can defer CGT on a gain if you invest an amount in EIS qualifying shares within the window 1 year before to 3 years after the disposal date. Deferral amount is effectively unlimited (subject to qualifying conditions). CGT becomes payable when the EIS shares are disposed of (or certain events occur). 
GOV.UK
Techzone

SEIS Reinvestment Relief: If you invest in SEIS shares, 50% of the amount invested (up to £200k invested) can be exempt from CGT on the original gain (i.e., it reduces the gain, it doesn’t defer it). 
GOV.UK
+1

Inputs

Mode: EIS Deferral | SEIS Reinvestment (50% exemption)

originalGain (£)

subscription (£) (amount invested in EIS/SEIS)

dateOfDisposal (date) (date the original gain arose)

dateOfShareIssue (date) (date EIS/SEIS shares were issued)

Your CGT rate (10% / 20%) (for illustrations of tax deferred/saved)

Calculations

Common checks

Window test (EIS only): dateOfShareIssue must be within [disposal−1y, disposal+3y]. If false → show red validation and set deferrable to £0. 
GOV.UK

A) EIS Deferral mode

Deferrable amount
deferrable = min(originalGain, subscription) (no statutory cap on amount deferred) 
Techzone

CGT deferred (illustration)
cgtDeferred = deferrable × chosenCGTRate

Earliest re-trigger date
For the illustration, show: “CGT becomes chargeable when you dispose of the EIS shares (or on a disqualifying event). You must hold ≥3 years.” (show dateOfShareIssue + 3y as the minimum hold period marker). 
Techzone

B) SEIS Reinvestment mode (50% exemption)

Eligible investment cap: eligible = min(subscription, 200000) (invested amount) 
GOV.UK

Gain exempted: gainExempt = min(originalGain, eligible × 50%)

CGT saved: cgtSaved = gainExempt × chosenCGTRate

Gain remaining: remainingGain = originalGain − gainExempt

Validation & assumptions

We do not model detailed share identification rules or partial disposals.

If EIS window test fails → show helper: “EIS deferral window is 1y before to 3y after the disposal date.” 
GOV.UK

If subscription > originalGain in EIS mode, we still cap deferrable to the gain.

If SEIS: remind user SEIS is exemption (not deferral) and is limited to £200k invested → £100k gain exempt. 
GOV.UK

Outputs

Headline: “Estimated CGT deferred/saved: £X”

Breakdown: Original gain; Amount matched; Rate used; Exempted/Deferred; Remaining gain (SEIS) or “Deferred until disposal” (EIS)

Timeline stub (EIS): Disposal → Share issue (✓ window?) → Minimum hold 3 years → “CGT due on disposal”

Worked examples

EIS Deferral
Gain £150,000; invest £120,000; CGT 20%; share issue 6 months after disposal.
Deferrable = £120,000; CGT deferred = £24,000; Minimum hold date = issue+3 years.

SEIS Reinvestment
Gain £180,000; invest £160,000 in SEIS; CGT 20%.
Eligible = £160,000 (≤200k); Gain exempt = 50% × 160k = £80,000; CGT saved = £16,000; Remaining gain = £100,000.
