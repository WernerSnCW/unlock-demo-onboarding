Step 7 — Non-advice guardrails (must be implemented)
A. Rename and frame Step 7 correctly

Do not call it “Target Portfolio” on-screen if you can avoid it. “Target” implies prescription.

Preferred UI titles (choose one):

“Illustrative Portfolio Direction”

“Proposed Portfolio Shape”

“Portfolio Scenario (Illustrative)”

And the subtitle must state:

“This is an illustrative analysis based on the information provided. It is not financial advice.”

B. Explicit “what this is / isn’t” box (always visible)

Above the charts, show a short boxed disclaimer:

This page provides an illustrative portfolio scenario.
It reflects the constraints and preferences captured in onboarding.
It does not account for the full suitability assessment required for regulated advice, and it does not instruct you to buy or sell any asset.

C. Language constraints (hard rules)
Ban these words/phrases everywhere in Step 7:

“recommend”, “we recommend”

“should”, “you should”

“best”, “optimal”

“must”

“buy”, “sell”, “switch”, “move your money”, “reduce your holding in X”

“to improve returns”

“to minimise tax” (unless phrased as “tax-aware modelling”)

Approved alternatives:

“Unlock models an illustrative scenario…”

“This reflects your stated preferences…”

“This constrains changes to remain within guardrails…”

“This highlights trade-offs…”

“A common consideration in this situation is…”

“This can be used as a starting point for discussion…”

D. Output structure: show ranges and trade-offs, not instructions

Instead of “set equity to 60%”, do:

Ranges (e.g. Equity: 45–55% illustrative band)

Or “directional arrows” (“equity risk: neutral / slightly lower / slightly higher”)

Instead of “reduce concentration”, do:

“Concentration: modelled to remain within guardrails”

“Concentration: constrained (cannot apply some tilts without increasing concentration risk)”

E. “Choice architecture” (optional but very good)

Offer 2–3 labelled illustrative scenarios:

Guardrail-first (Default) – prioritises bringing lights to green

Preference-leaning (Constrained) – applies tilts as far as budgets allow

Neutral baseline – minimal deviation from current

This avoids “we’re telling you what to do” because you’re showing options within constraints.
