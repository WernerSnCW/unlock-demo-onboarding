Pitch Deck Analyser – Enhanced Specification
🎯 Purpose

Analyse startup pitch decks to:

Extract key information.

Benchmark against investor expectations.

Provide valuation estimates.

Generate investor questions for due diligence.

Give strengths & weaknesses by section.

🔹 1. Input

Upload PDF/PPTX deck.

Optional: sector, stage, geography.

🔹 2. Processing Modules
A. Section Identification & Analysis

Tool scans for the 9–10 standard pitch deck sections.
For each section:

Presence: Detected / Missing.

Strengths: What’s clearly articulated (e.g., “Strong TAM data with credible sources”).

Weaknesses: Gaps or unclear points (e.g., “No revenue model explained”).

Investor Questions: Auto-generated prompts.

Example (Business Model Section):

Strength: Clear SaaS subscription model with tiered pricing.

Weakness: No unit economics or customer acquisition cost.

Suggested Questions:

“What is your current CAC and how do you project it will change?”

“How long is your average customer retention?”

B. Content Completeness

% of standard sections covered.

Radar chart showing which are strong vs weak.

C. Clarity & Consistency

NLP checks for clarity vs jargon.

Detect contradictions across slides.

Score out of 10.

D. Financial & Market Sanity

Extracted financials compared to stage & sector benchmarks.

Highlight unrealistic assumptions (burn multiple, revenue CAGR).

Output: benchmark chart + “plausibility flag”.

E. Valuation Analysis

Quick Benchmark: Compare stage & sector medians.

Basic Model: Apply revenue/EBITDA multiples.

Output: Estimated range vs declared ask.

Pro Option: Adjustable inputs for custom scenario.

F. Risk Indicators

Automatically flag missing or weak areas:

No competition slide.

No exit strategy.

No traction metrics.

Assign severity level (⚠️ Low, 🚩 Medium, ❌ High).

🔹 3. Outputs & UX
A. Executive Summary

Overall score (Completeness, Clarity, Valuation Plausibility).

Top 3 strengths.

Top 3 weaknesses.

Top 5 investor questions to ask.

B. Section-by-Section Deep Dive

For each section:

Status: Present / Missing.

Strengths.

Weaknesses.

Suggested investor questions.

Example Output:

Market Size (TAM/SAM/SOM):

Strength: Includes TAM figure with source (PwC 2023).

Weakness: No clear SAM or SOM breakdown.

Investor Questions:

“How do you validate your SAM figure beyond top-down estimates?”

“What % of SOM do you realistically aim to capture in 3 years?”

C. Valuation Snapshot

“Declared valuation: £10m”

“Benchmark range: £4.2–6.8m”

“Overvalued by ~35% compared to peers.”

D. Report Modes

In-App Dashboard (Free): Interactive cards + charts.

Exportable PDF (Pro): Full deck breakdown, valuation charts, question bank.
